Why My Generation Can't Wait on Climate Change

Maya Rodriguez · English 9 · Ms. Patel · Riverview USD · April 17

Climate change is a big problem and we need to do something about it soon.My generation is going to have to live with every climate decision adults are making right now, which is why students like me can't afford to keep waiting quietly for them to act.Thesis Scientists have been warning us for years, but not enough has changed. The effects are already happening in places all around the world and people my age are starting to notice it more than ever. If we don't take action quickly, the future could look very different from what we are used to.

One reason climate change matters is because it affects our everyday lives. A lot of places are experiencing weather that is getting worse.Vague For example, my cousin lives in Phoenix and she told me last summer was the hottest one she can remember, with over fifty days above 110 degreesSpecific. That kind of heat isn't just uncomfortable — it's dangerous, especially for older people and kids who play outside. These are the kinds of things that used to feel far away but now feel close.

Another reason is that young people are the ones who will have to live with the decisions being made right now. Adults talk about climate change like it's a future problem, but for us it's a right-now problem. I've watched classmates skip school to go to climate marches, and I've seen the way my little brother asks questions about whether the planet will be okay when he grows up. That hit me hard.Raw We shouldn't have to grow up scared.Hero line

There are things we can do. Schools could teach more about sustainability, communities could invest in public transit, and governments could pass stronger laws about pollution. I know some of this is already happening but it needs to happen faster. Every year we wait is a year we can't get back.Supporting

In conclusion, climate change is something that can't be ignored anymoreIncomplete
